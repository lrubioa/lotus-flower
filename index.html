Lotus Flower

The lotus flower is a plant native to Asia and Australia, with a very large range. The flower is a aquatic perinnial, which means it grows in the water and is dormant in the winter, but can be seen throughout summer and autumn. It prefers to grow in shallow water and warm sunlight. Flower colors include blue, white, pink, purple and red. Each flower lasts for a few days at most, but many are produced. In many cultures and religions, the lotus flower represents purity, grace, beauty, and persistence, with how beautiful and clean it's petals are, comparedto its swampy habitat. Lotus flowers have many different uses, including medicine, art, food, and ornamental.

These flowers have also been prevalent in much of history. It has been shown that it has been alive for over 145 million years. It even survived the Ice Age, which ended the existence of so many other plant and animal species. The can survive under ice as long as their roots are in the mud. In the extreme heat, they are still also able to grow. They are resistant to pollution and can purify the water. In the night, they sink underwater and in the day, they rise and rebloom once more.
